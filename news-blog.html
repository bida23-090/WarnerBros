The Sandman's Constantine Actor Reacts To Keanu Reeves' Constantine 2

Grant Hermanns
Published 15 Hours Ago
As audiences continue to celebrate the film's development The Sandman's Constantine actor Jenna Coleman shares her reaction to Keanu Reeves' Constantine 2.

Coleman starred in Netflix's adaptation of Neil Gaiman's beloved comic book series as Johanna Constantine, a female version of the British occult detective who assists Tom Sturridge's Morpheus in reacquiring his totems of power.

Despite initial controversy over the switch from John to Johanna Constantine, Coleman ultimately scored strong reviews for her turn in The Sandman, with many eager to see more of her in the future.

Before Coleman returns, however, audiences can look forward to Keanu Reeves returning for Constantine 2, a sequel the actor has long desired to make that was recently confirmed to be in development with original director Francis Lawrence also returning. Warner Bros.'

Shift towards the new film ultimately resulted in the cancelation of the HBO Max reboot with His House's Sope Dirisu having been in talks to star. While the future of The Sandman remains unclear, one star is addressing Reeves stepping back into his cult favorite role.



Though Coleman may not offer a proper statement on Reeves' Constantine 2, her interest in seeing more of the occult detective on screen is sure to ring true with fans of the character. Even prior to The Sandman's release, Coleman had expressed her interest in leading a Johanna Constantine spinoff show, having recently also shared that Gaiman is similarly interested in making the project happen and is currently developing a possible idea with showrunner Allan Heinberg.

Given Netflix has yet to greenlight The Sandman season 2, it's understandable Coleman's Constantine spinoff may not ultimately get off the ground, though her devotion to the character speaks volumes for fans eager to see respectful portrayals of the beloved character.

Wonder Woman 4 Teased By Patty Jenkins Before Third DCEU Movie Releases

Ellie Lecomber-Clark
Published 23 Hours Ago
Director Patty Jenkins teases the possibility of Wonder Woman 4, despite filming on Wonder Woman 3 having yet to begin. Having joined the DC Extended Universe back in 2016's Batman V Superman: Dawn of Justice, Gadot's Wonder Woman, otherwise known as Diana Prince, has already established her position as one of the DCEU's heavy hitters.

Her two standalone appearances, Wonder Woman and Wonder Woman 1984, have enjoyed considerable success with their respective 2017 and 2020 releases.

While the audience's reception of Wonder Woman 1984 was somewhat less favorable when compared to that of its predecessor, it wasn't long before confirmation came that Wonder Woman 3 was in the works, with Jenkins set to return once again to round out her trilogy.

While details surrounding Wonder Woman 3 have been sparse, small updates have been provided sporadically from both the director and Gadot herself, with both having expressed their mutual excitement to continue Diana's story. Earlier this year, Gadot shared that Wonder Woman 3 could begin filming at some point in 2023, though any official timeline for the film's production or release has yet to be confirmed.

During a recent appearance at Matera Film Festival, Jenkins opened up about how each Wonder Woman film has spurred on her desire to continue Diana's story further, despite having initially only planned to do one film.

While confirming that she now has a completed script for Wonder Woman 3, the director shared that this trend had continued, as she is now interested in exploring Diana's story past the events of this third installment. Jenkins quickly added that she has a number of other projects on the horizon that she is also excited to work on, though this quick tease of the possibility of Wonder Woman 4 did not go unnoticed. Check out Jenkins' full quote below.



Given the success of the previous Wonder Woman films, it’s likely that DC is far from finished with the exploration of Diana Prince’s story, even despite the fact that former Warner Bros. chairman Toby Emmerich previously stated that Wonder Woman 3 will serve as the finale of Gadot and Jenkins' "long-planned" Wonder Woman trilogy.

This is not the first time that Jenkins has teased that she has more stories in mind for just how Diana’s story could continue on past the events of Wonder Woman 3, although similarly to these new comments, Jenkins also previously stated that these may or may not happen under her direction.

No Way Home Ned Actor Shares Emotional Reaction To MCU Movie's Ending

Connor Williams
Published 4 Days Ago
Tom Holland and Jacob Batalon in Spider Man No Way Home ending Spider-Man: No Way Home star Jacob Batalon shares his emotional reaction to being part of the MCU and potentially saying goodbye to his character Ned Leeds after Spidey's most recent outing. Releasing late in 2021, Spider-Man: No Way Home not only closed the narrative arcs of Tom Holland's first trilogy as Marvel's web-swinging icon, but also culminated in the joining of three generations worth of the titular character.

To the delights of moviegoers, Tobey Maguire and Andrew Garfield both reprized their roles as different iterations of Spider-Man, allying with Holland to face off against fellow franchise veterans Willem Dafoe, Alfred Molina and Jamie Foxx.

Though it was not only the heroes of an older on-screen generation that aided Holland's Spidey in his biggest test yet, but also Batalon's wise-cracking and magically gifted best-friend Ned, alongside the equally bold and resourceful Michelle "MJ" Jones played by Zendaya.

This triad of the titular hero backed up by his unwaveringly loyal companions was already one of the more popular in the MCU; making them part of the wider arcs of returning characters from previously beloved generations of the comic-book movie genre only increased this popularity.

However, after the emotional ending of Spider-Man: No Way Home, in which the entire world's memory of Peter Parker was erased, it is yet to be seen whether the trio will ever reunite.



While it hasn't been officially added to the release calendar yet, Kevin Feige confirmed the MCU's Spider-Man 4 is in development, with Holland being eyed to reprise the titular role again. Sony is expecting director Jon Watts and Zendaya to return for Spider-Man 4 as well, but nothing's been confirmed.

Given the success of the Spider-Man: Homecoming trilogy, it's understandable why the studio would be interested in keeping the core team intact. Holland's chemistry with Batalon and Zendaya served as the emotional core of Spider-Man's solo MCU outings, and viewers would enjoy seeing them onscreen again.

However, a quick reunion between the three would run the risk of undoing Spider-Man: No Way Home's poignant ending. As Batalon notes, No Way Home's conclusion could allow for more Spider-Man adventures with a different supporting cast. A Spider-Man 4 could still be a ways off however, as Marvel makes its way through the Multiverse Saga.

Bowser Is Already The Best Thing About The Super Mario Bros. Movie

Henry Austin
Published 19 Hours Ago
With the release of the trailer, The Super Mario Bros. Movie is already a divisive topic, with exception to Bowser who's shaped up to be the best thing about the film. The first trailer for The Super Mario Bros.

Movie has given a glimpse into Nintendo and Illumination's animated adaptation of the classic video game franchise. The movie continues the recent string of video game adaptations, with Detective Pikachu, Sonic the Hedgehog, and Uncharted all proving to be popular and generally performing well at the global box office.

Controversially, rather than using or imitating the Nintendo character's voices from the video games, The Super Mario Bros. Movie cast is made up of Hollywood's biggest stars and most familiar voices. Similar to how devoted SEGA fans responded to the original "ugly Sonic" design for 2020's Sonic the Hedgehog, long-time Nintendo players have reacted negatively to Chris Pratt's voice for Mario. The Super Mario Bros.

Movie trailer only gives Mario a couple of lines, but both sound exactly like Pratt, a dramatic change from the character's famously distinctive Italian accent. Despite the backlash to Chris Pratt's Mario voice, the trailer suggests that one Super Mario character has been adapted perfectly for the movie.

Bowser, Mario's greatest enemy in the video games, looks set to be the best part of The Super Mario Bros. Movie. Voiced by the legendary Jack Black, Bowser is the focus of the movie's first trailer, and he has already been painted out to be a ruthless monster.

Bowser's look is a faithful take on his video game design, but arguably, and more importantly, the energy and characterization that Black brings to the role are in keeping with the Bowser that Nintendo fans know and love. Unlike Pratt's uninspired voice work, it's clear that veteran voice actor Jack Black knows exactly what he wants Bowser to be. The actor has successfully captured the King of the Koopas video-game aggression while injecting elements of his own fun personality.

Encanto: Why Casita Can't Save Mirabel In Bruno's Secret Hideaway

Mariah Magyugh
Published 7 Days Ago
In Disney's 2021 film, Encanto, the magical house Casita fails to protect the primary protagonist, Mirabel, even when Mirabel goes on a quest to save Casita from falling apart. Casita isn't thoughtless or careless, though, and has good reason for not helping Mirabel at certain points in the film.

Mirabel, a young woman who doesn't get powers in a family where every member receives a special gift on their fifth birthday, wants to prove herself worthy to her family. The family resides in a sentient house, lovingly nicknamed Casita, who often helps Mirabel throughout the film. However, when Mirabel is exploring Bruno's hidden room and the walls that he hides behind, Casita's help is noticeably absent.

When Mirabel is on her quest to figure out what is making Casita crumble and why her family is losing their magical powers, she sets off to find Bruno, her uncle. When Mirabel was younger, he left the family to protect her because he received the gift of prophecy but Bruno's visions always seemed to be bad news.

Each member of the Madrigal family has a special room tailored to them that Casita creates for them when they get their magical power. Throughout the film, only two rooms besides Bruno's are seen and both times Casita is able to use its magic within the bedrooms.

When Mirabel was in Bruno's room, it was full of sand and hard to navigate, but Casita offered no assistance, even when the room started to collapse. Casita also fails to protect Mirabel when she nearly dies while chasing Bruno inside the walls of the house.

Casita is concealing a secret, Bruno, and the hidden spaces within the house provide an opportunity to conceal that secret. Because Bruno has stepped away from the family, the light on his door is dim, indicating that Casita's magic doesn't work in there anymore. Just like any other room, though, it was once enchanted and Bruno's room may serve as a metaphor for the heartbreak he endured trying to protect Mirabel.

Much like any other member of the Madrigal family, Casita has its own pressures and desires. Casita is bound together by the Madrigals' love for one another and relies on their unity to stay alive, much like they rely on Casita for its magic. Casita also doesn't hesitate to knock some sense into them when they are misbehaving.

For example, Casita lets Mirabel walk into a pole early in the film after she snaps at her sister. When Casita sees how the Madrigals, especially Abuela, are treating Bruno it makes the decision to conceal Bruno by dismantling its own magic and giving Bruno a safe place to retreat.

The Marvels Tease Sets Up An Even More Powerful Captain Marvel

James Eddy
Published 2 Weeks Ago
Captain Marvel has already displayed her immense powers in the Marvel Cinematic Universe, but a recent interview has teased the possibility of her being even more powerful in The Marvels.

Since making her onscreen debut in Captain Marvel, Carol Danvers/Captain Marvel (Brie Larson) has made limited appearances in the MCU, appearing only in Avengers: Endgame, the mid-credit scene in Shang-Chi and the Legend of the Ten Rings, and in a brief cameo in Ms. Marvel. Perhaps, inevitably, this will change with the release of The Marvels, where she will be joined by her superfan Kamala Khan/Ms. Marvel (Iman Vellani) and her best friend’s daughter Monica Rambeau (Teyonah Parris).

Details on what to expect from the Nia DaCosta-directed sequel have been limited. However, Fast and Furious 10 cast member, Brie Larson, has suggested that The Marvels will show an uneasy team-up between the three main characters.

This unease was caused, in part, by the anger Rambeau had shown towards Danvers in Wandavision, and the swapping of powers which was hinted at by Captain Marvel’s brief appearance in Ms. Marvel and in footage from The Marvels revealed at the D23 Expo.

In addition, Brie Larson has hinted at the possibility of her character’s powers being expanded in comparison to what audiences have seen before. In an interview, she described the physical strain that training for The Marvels had taken before adding, “what's different with this movie from the first one is that Carol didn't fly until the end, so I didn't have as much wire work stuff” (via EW).



This certainly suggests that Carol Danvers is fully embracing her powers. After all, Captain Marvel was an origin story in which she didn’t do much flying and while her most powerful moment in Avengers: Endgame involved her flying in and single-handedly destroying Thanos' Sanctuary II flagship, her screentime was still rather limited.

As a result, the implication that Captain Marvel will spend a significant amount of time in flight during The Marvels may suggest that the movie would show off how powerful she had become and push her powers further than had been seen before.
